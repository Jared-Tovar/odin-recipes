Skillet Chicken Bulgogi

Photo by Dotdash Meredith Food Studios

This bulgogi chicken is a quick-and-easy, but very tasty, meal. You can substitute the chicken with beef or pork for variety. My wife and I absolutely love this recipe, and it only takes 30 minutes with very few dirty dishes! Serve over rice.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup chopped onion
  • 5 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 ½ tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cut into thin strips

Directions

  1. Combine onion, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, sesame oil, sesame seeds, cayenne, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Place chicken in a large resealable plastic bag.
  3. Pour 1/2 of the marinade into the bag.
  4. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. Set remaining marinade aside.
  6. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  7. Remove chicken from the bag and discard marinade.
  8. Cook and stir chicken in the skillet until no longer pink in the center, 4 to 6 minutes.
  9. Pour reserved marinade over chicken.
  10. Continue to cook and stir until chicken is coated and cooked through, 5 to 7 minutes.

Cook's Note

You can substitute peanut oil for the sesame oil if desired. Add vegetables if you wish; I normally use carrot straws and sliced mushrooms. For a little more bite, I use a peppercorn medley grinder instead of black pepper.
